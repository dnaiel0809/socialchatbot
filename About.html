Study Policy

Conditions that will result in rejection:

1. Please take this study seriously. We expect you to concentrate on the chatting task for about 15 - 25 mins per day. It is highly recommended that spending at least 15 minutes for each day's session, since some questions might be tricky and need more attention than others.

2. Please avoid gibberish or short responses.

(1) Avoid general answers, such as "the person needs to see the doctor", since most of the questions are based on different contexts and cannot be addressed simply by a vague answer.

(2) Avoid passive responses, such as "I don't know" and "I'm not sure". Please keep in mind that there is no right or wrong answer. It is important that you provide your thoughts truthfully.

(3) Avoid too short answers. A short answer is defined by a sentence with less than ten words. Providing a short answer might result in rejection.

(4) Avoid off-topic answers. An off-topic answer is defined as an answer that has no direct relations with what the chatbot is talking/asking for.

3. Please avoid chatting with the chatbot with languages other than English.

Conditions that will result in participation ineligibility:

1. The task will be sent out on a daily basis, thus, failing to complete the task for more than 1 day will automatically result in your ineligibility to further participate in this study. 

You will receive a chatting task from the conversational agent at 2 - 3 pm (Eastern Standard Time (EST))/11 - 12 pm (Pacific Standard Time (PST)) every day, and you will finish each chatting task by 02:59 am (EST)/11:59 pm (PST).

Please note that you will NOT get opportunities for remedy if you miss tasks on 2nd, 4th, 6th, 8th, 10th, 12th, and 14th day.

2. If your answer is rejected for more than 1 time, you will not be eligible to further participate in the study.

Other policies:

1. The chatting tasks' questions and loading may vary by day. Please do your best to answer the chatbot every day.

2. Please take the initiative to speak to the chatbot by after 3 pm (Eastern Standard Time (EST)) \12 pm (Pacific Standard Time (PST)). Send a message (Hi, Hey ... etc.) to the chatbot and see if you can get the task from the chatbot.

3. Please avoid asking questions to the chatbot.

Our chatbot system is not very intelligent, so it needs some of your understanding and accommodation. Asking questions is not necessary for the study and the chatbot might not understand your questions.

4. Please avoid separating one answer into multiple responses. 

Separating your answer into multiple responses will make your answer invalid and cause more confusion for the chatbot.
